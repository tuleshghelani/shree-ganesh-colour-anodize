Our Advanced Chrome Plating Process

1. Surface Preparation

Meticulous cleaning and preparation of metal surfaces to remove contaminants and ensure optimal chrome adhesion and finish quality.

2. Base Layer Application

For decorative applications, precision application of copper and nickel underlayers to enhance corrosion resistance and surface quality.

3. Chrome Electroplating

Controlled immersion in chromium solution with precise electrical current application to deposit a uniform chrome layer of the specified thickness.

4. Post-Plating Treatment

Specialized rinsing and neutralizing processes to remove residual chemicals and stabilize the chrome surface for optimal performance.

5. Quality Assurance

Comprehensive testing and inspection to verify plating thickness, adhesion, appearance, and performance characteristics meet all specifications.

Frequently Asked Questions About Chrome Plating

What is chrome plating?

›

Chrome plating is an electroplating process that applies a thin layer of chromium onto a metal surface. This creates a durable, corrosion-resistant finish with a distinctive mirror-like appearance that enhances both aesthetics and performance.

How durable is chrome plating?

›

Chrome plating is exceptionally durable, offering superior hardness and wear resistance. Properly plated chrome surfaces can withstand years of use in demanding environments, resisting scratches, corrosion, and chemical damage while maintaining their distinctive appearance.

What metals can be chrome plated?

›

Most metals can be chrome plated, including steel, aluminum, copper, brass, and zinc alloys. Each base metal requires specific preparation techniques to ensure proper adhesion and finish quality. Our experts can advise on the best approach for your specific components.

What is the difference between decorative and hard chrome plating?

›

Decorative chrome plating is thinner (0.2-0.5 microns) and primarily used for aesthetic purposes with underlying nickel layers for corrosion protection. Hard chrome plating is thicker (2-250 microns), applied directly to the base metal, and designed for industrial applications requiring extreme wear resistance and durability.

How do I maintain chrome-plated surfaces?

›

Maintain chrome-plated surfaces by regularly cleaning with mild soap and water, avoiding abrasive cleaners or tools that can scratch the finish. For automotive applications, apply a quality chrome polish periodically. Prevent prolonged exposure to harsh chemicals and salt, and address any damage promptly to prevent corrosion.
